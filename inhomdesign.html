The answer can be found in the April 13, 2016 Economic Roundup published by Furniture Today, the leading publication for the furniture industry. The web, Internet as much discussed is now the most integral part of any person’s day to day activity. Now is also becoming an intrinsic part of consumer’s path for purchasing home furniture. By every metric the influence of the Internet grows daily and paralleling increasing the use of smartphones into consumer’s lifestyles. This trend shows upward trajectory for online home and furniture shopping. Online research is also an integral part of consumer’s shopping with (55% of upholstery buyers, 69% of case goods buyers and 71% of area rug buyers) which states that consumers research online before shopping. This clearly states that even if 45% were NOT interested in purchasing upholstery online due to obvious reasons (size, shipping expenses, return hassles, etc.) on the other side, 55% do venture into online shopping for the same. Similarly, for the other items. This clearly shows that Internet can be a holding factor and an integral source for any home and furniture shopping success. Online shopping can prove very useful as it does not just expose a wide variety, colors size, trends, etc. but also gives consumers flexibility to choose and compare everything in one place and save time and hassle going to multiple stores for doing the same. All the existing and upcoming furniture retail stores now have their own online website or an app that enables user to shop the furniture online by looking at the variety and comparing everything sitting in one place.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Importantly, I studied some existing apps in the market to understand what already works and what might be lacking in them.
The fact that a lot of people do love keeping their home beautiful but still approach traditional way of shopping for it showed the absence of apps/services that have been successful in tackling this problem.
This also helped me understand various prospects of my idea from a business standpoint.
I chose 2 apps Houzz and Wayfair to conduct a competitive analysis and identify the strengths, weaknesses and opportunities for improvement.

REVENUE MODEL

Hybrid Model which consists of (Dropship + Referral)

  1. Drop ship- Retail fulfillment method (smaller suppliers – NorCal/ KingFuton) -
    Store does not keep the products it sells in stock. Instead, when a store sells a product, it purchases the item from a third party and has it shipped directly to the customer. As a result, the merchant never has to handle 3rd party redirection.
  2. Referral -
    My app will be paid certain % of purchase because customer was referred to the specific store through my app. (Crate & Barrel, Macy’s, Pier1, Walmart) - stores that are not primarily into furniture market.

BENEFITS

  • Managing or paying for a warehouse
  • Packing and shipping your orders
  • Tracking inventory for accounting reasons
  • Handling returns and inbound shipments
  • Continually ordering products and managing stock level
  • Less overhead as we don’t have to deal with the inventory
  • Wide selection of products

  • The largest projects in terms of average spend in the last five years were custom home builds ($577,000), complete home remodels ($193,000).
